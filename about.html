Who I am

A multimedia producer and archaeologist with a passion for making the ancient world viscerally present.

My practice sits at the boundary between rigorous archaeological research and experiential storytelling. I work across immersive media, documentary filmmaking, and heritage communication — finding new languages to bring the past into the present.

Currently I lead research at Visualising Heritage, University of Bradford, where I develop innovative approaches to communicating heritage through 360° film, VR, projection mapping, and photogrammetry. Previously, I produced documentary content for CNN and National Geographic, telling character-driven stories with global reach.
